1. OBJECTIVE AND SCOPE
===============================================================================

This document compiles all the regression tests and checks explicitly proposed
in the conclusions of the ten games from the
PHILOCHESS 0.2-alpha-001 Validation: No Syzygy tournament.

The inventory initially retains every case, even when several of them test the
same general principle. Two different positions are not considered duplicates
merely because they share the same tactical motif.

The FEN positions were reconstructed by legally replaying the complete PGNs.
When a test comprises several positions involving check, all FEN positions
identified in the analysis are included.

This inventory does not yet specify:
- the final depth for each normal-search test;
- exact evaluation values when the analysis establishes only a qualitative
  relationship;
- strict limits on quiescence nodes;
- a single best move when several correct alternatives may exist.

Those parameters must be determined when the inventory is converted into the
executable PHILOCHESS 0.2-alpha-002 test suite.

===============================================================================
2. CLASSIFICATION
===============================================================================

[A] ALPHA-002 — QUIESCENCE SEARCH
    Tests suitable for qprobe or for a new internal quiescence-regression
    command. They cover captures, promotions, complete check evasions, the
    prohibition of stand pat while in check, and forced continuations supported
    by the current set of quiescence moves.

[B] ALPHA-002 — NORMAL SEARCH + QUIESCENCE SEARCH
    Tests containing quiet moves, non-capturing checks, threats, material
    sacrifices followed by maneuvers, or mating sequences that the current
    quiescence search is not expected to solve in isolation. They must be run
    through the normal search, which will use quiescence search upon reaching
    the horizon.

[C] LATER VERSIONS — POSITIONAL EVALUATION
    Tests whose successful completion depends primarily on development, mobility,
    activity, structure, king safety, coordination, or other knowledge that is
    not yet present. They are retained now, but in alpha-002 they can be used
    only as baselines for legality, stability, and measurement.

ROUND 1 — Zoidberg 0.1 vs PHILOCHESS 0.2-alpha-001
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

R01-T01 [C] Position before 8...c5
Starting position: after 8.O-O; Black to move.
FEN: rnb1kbnr/1pqppppp/2p5/8/p2PP3/N4N2/PPPBBPPP/R2Q1RK1 b kq - 7 8
Objective: verify that 8...c5 relinquishes control of b5, allows 9.Nb5, and
facilitates the knight's subsequent entry on c7.
Criterion: the future positional evaluation must compare 8...c5 with alternatives
that retain control of b5 and favor development and king safety. In alpha-002,
this will be retained only as a baseline.

R01-T02 [B] Position before 12...Qxe4
Starting position: after 12.Ne5; Black to move.
FEN: rnb1kbnr/1p1ppppp/2q5/1N2N3/p3P3/4B3/PPP1BPPP/R2Q1RK1 b kq - 3 12
Objective: verify that the capture 12...Qxe4 is not evaluated as a clean gain
and that the search reaches 13.Nc7+, with a double attack on the king and rook.
Criterion: recognize the tactical consequence without necessarily classifying
12...Qxe4 as an independent error, since the black queen already has no
satisfactory retreat that keeps c7 protected.

R01-T03 [B] Position before 16...f6
Starting position: after 16.Nb6; Black to move.
FEN: rnbk1bnr/1p1p1ppp/1N2p3/4N3/p7/4B3/PPP1BPPP/R4RK1 b - - 1 16
Objective: verify the threat and consequence of 16...f6 17.Nf7+ Ke8 18.Nxh8.
Criterion: the search must recognize the double attack and loss of the rook,
without presenting 16...f6 as the sole cause of a position that was already
deteriorated.

4. CROSS-CUTTING TESTS DERIVED FROM THE INVENTORY
===============================================================================

The following checks do not replace the preceding 64 cases. They group properties
that must be verified in several positions to prevent isolated implementation
errors.

TR-01 — Stand pat prohibited while in check
Sets: R03-T05, R04-T04, R05-T03/T05, R06-T07, R07-T04/T08,
R08-T05/T07, R09-T09, and R10-T07.
This must hold at every in-check node, not only at the qprobe root.

TR-02 — Complete evasion generation
Verify blocks, captures of the attacking piece, and king moves.
Compare the generated set with the expected legal set for each FEN.

TR-03 — Chained captures through stability
Sets: R03-T03/T04, R04-T01/T03, R08-T04, R09-T03/T09.
Do not stop the evaluation at an apparent gain when a legal recapture or
counterattack exists within the quiescence set.

TR-04 — Promotions
Sets: R06-T05/T06 and R08-T06/T07.
Include non-capturing promotions, promotions by capture, promotions with check,
and all four legal promotion pieces.

TR-05 — Move ordering without altering the chess outcome
Run selected positions with and without move ordering within quiescence search.
The acceptable best move and evaluation must coincide; only nodes and cutoffs
should change.

TR-06 — Quiescence-search growth
Record for each case:
- main nodes;
- quiescence nodes;
- qnodes/nodes ratio;
- quiescence beta cutoffs;
- quiescence nodes started while in check;
- selective depth;
- 64-ply limit hits.
Do not yet set a universal node ceiling; identify outlier positions and compare
growth before and after each correction.

TR-07 — 64-ply limit
All cases must end with zero hits under normal conditions. If a hit occurs, the
test must record the FEN, line, and reason before determining whether an error or
an exceptional position exists.

TR-08 — Mate scores
Sets: R07-T07/T09, R09-T08/T10, and R10-T05/T06.
Verify sign, distance, preference for the shortest mate, and resistance by
choosing the longest mating line. This test belongs to normal search +
quiescence search.

TR-09 — Deliberate limit of the current quiescence search
Sets: R05-T04, R07-T07, R09-T08, and R10-T06/T08.
Document that non-capturing checks and other quiet moves are not part of qprobe
in alpha-001. Do not declare a quiescence-search failure when the combination can
only be discovered from the normal search, unless alpha-002 expressly changes
that design.

TR-10 — Preservation of positive capabilities
Main sets: R03-T01, R08-T01, R08-T02, R08-T03, and the tactical resources in
R09-T04/T06.
The alpha-002 corrections must not eliminate defenses or resources that
alpha-001 already found correctly.

TR-11 — General legality and stability
For each FEN:
- correct loading of the position;
- generation of legal moves only;
- make and unmake moves without board corruption;
- output of a legal move;
- no freeze, exception, or unexpected termination;
- clean termination within the time limit.

TR-12 — Independent perft regression
Repeat perftknown 4 with its 24/24 correct values to confirm that the quiescence-
search corrections do not alter legal move generation, castling, en passant
captures, promotions, check detection, or king safety.

Phase 1 — [A] cases
First implement the 21 quiescence-search tests and checks TR-01 through TR-07.

Phase 2 — [B] cases
Add the 33 normal-search + quiescence-search tests. Determine the minimum stable

Phase 3 — [C] cases
Retain the 10 positional positions as a future corpus. Do not require the purely
material evaluation of alpha-002 to prefer a positional move that it cannot yet
understand.

Phase 4 — Full execution
Run the inventory tests, qtest, tactictest, perftknown, UCI time-control tests,
and clean-exit tests. Record the results and any new case discovered during
development.

===============================================================================
7. CONCLUSION
===============================================================================

The analyses of the ten games provide 64 documented tests. The inventory shows
that not all the problems observed must be resolved in PHILOCHESS 0.2-alpha-002:
some belong to future positional evaluation.

The immediate objective of alpha-002 should be to convert the [A] and [B] cases
into a reproducible test suite that verifies correctness, stability, controlled
growth, and absence of regressions. The [C] cases are retained so that later
versions can demonstrate positional improvements on exactly the same real
tournament positions.
